About Me
Nicola Carroll - BSoc Sci, MCouns

Full member of the New Zealand Counsellors Association. (MNZAC)

I am a qualified experienced counsellor offering face to face and online counselling services. After graduating with a Masters in Counselling (first class honours) from the University of Waikato in 2012, I have worked as a counsellor for Hospice Tairawhiti supporting patients and whanau as they deal with a life limiting illness. Alongside this work I have been in private practice working with young people, older people, families and couples, working with anxiety, depression, grief loss and life transitions, relationship issues, effects of family violence, chronic illnesses, sexuality and gender identity.

How I Work

I am trained as a Narrative therapist which is a respectful, non-blaming approach to counselling and centres people as the experts in their own lives. We view problems as separate from people and assumes people have many skills, competencies, beliefs, values, commitments and abilities that will assist them to reduce the influence of problems in their lives.

Confidentiality

Everything you talk about in your counselling session is confidential. The exceptions to this is when somebody’s safety is at risk in which case I am ethically bound to take appropriate action. This usually involves talking with you first and trying to reach an agreement on what action to take, but sometimes this is not possible and I need to take action without talking to you first. I practice according to the New Zealand Association of Counsellors Code of ethics.

Consultation

Consultation can be done via Skype, Zoom or Face Time if you do not live locally or cannot travel to my consultation rooms.
